Cricket Rules

Basics of Cricket

The Game and Its Players: Cricket is a bat-and-ball game played between two teams, with each team consisting of eleven players.
Objective: The objective of the game is to score runs while also dismissing the opposing team's players.
Playing Field: The cricket field is oval-shaped, and the pitch is the rectangular area in the center where most of the action takes place.

Scoring Runs

Running Between Wickets: Batsmen can score runs by running between the two wickets.
Boundaries: If the ball crosses the boundary without touching the ground, the batting team is awarded four runs (boundary). If it clears the boundary on the full, it is worth six runs (six).
Extras: Runs can also be scored through extras, such as no-balls, wides, and byes.

Dismissal Methods

Wickets and Dismissal: The primary aim of the bowling team is to dismiss the batsmen. This can be achieved through various methods.
Bowled: If the bowler hits the stumps, dislodging the bails, the batsman is bowled out.
Caught: If the batsman hits the ball and it is caught by a fielder without bouncing, the batsman is dismissed as caught.

Bowling and Fielding Restrictions

Bowling Rules: Bowlers have limitations on the number of overs they can bowl in limited-overs cricket formats.
Powerplays: In limited-overs matches, there are powerplay periods during which fielding restrictions apply, allowing for more aggressive batting.
Fielding Positions: Different fielding positions are employed strategically to aid the bowlers and prevent scoring opportunities for the batsmen.
